
##  📌 Overview

This project analyzes screen time patterns among Indian children aged 8–18 years, focusing on how demographic factors, device usage, and lifestyle choices influence digital habits. With the rapid growth of smartphones, laptops, and online entertainment, children are spending increasing amounts of time on screens, often exceeding recommended limits.

The dataset used in this project captures key attributes such as age, gender, average daily screen time, primary device preference, educational vs recreational usage ratio, health impacts (poor sleep, eye strain, anxiety, obesity risk), and urban vs rural background. By cleaning and preprocessing the data with Pandas, and visualizing insights using Matplotlib, the project highlights important trends and concerns.

The analysis includes:

Gender distribution (bar and pie charts)

Age group spread (histogram)

Device usage trends (pie and bar charts by gender)

Health impacts of screen time (pie charts)

Urban vs rural differences (pie chart)

Key findings reveal that smartphones are the most used device, poor sleep is the most common health issue, and urban kids show higher screen time compared to rural peers.

This project serves as a foundation for further exploration, including predictive modeling of health risks, correlation with academic performance, and regional comparisons across India. It provides a practical example of how data science can be applied to real-world social issues.
